NO-CODE CIRCUITS

PUSHBUTTON LED
Our first exploration into arduino began in completing simple circuits, with the power source being only a battery - no code. Even this was new to me, so i was in for a lot of learning. Our first circuit was a simple push button, turning on a LED. I was surprised how finnicky making the circuits were, and how attentive you have to be to positive and negative currents. The LED bulb wont work unless the long end is attached to positive current, for example.

2PUSHBUTTON_2LED
Quite quickly, Rebekah and I got the hang of it and began figuring out our own circuits, based on what we had already done. These images show our experiment with two push buttons and two LEDS, on the left we just used one buytton to active two LEDs, and below we used two buttons each turning on their corresponding LED's. After being concerned i wouldn't figure it out, our teamwork was very useful to build my confidence

POTENTIOMETER LED
Our final no-code circuit was an extension on how we could manipulate an LED. Using a potentiometer (a dial), we were able to succesfully create a circuit that adjusted the brightness of the LED. These circuits gave me confidence in my ability to create circuits that worked, which was an incredibly useful starting point for the rest of the physucal computing module. Any arduino.ide code wont work if the circuit is wrongly made.
