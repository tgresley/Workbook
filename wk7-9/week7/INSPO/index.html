physical computing projects

each of these takes movement as input
and computes it into a visual output

in so doing, we are encouraged to challenge our perceptions of movement,
and be conscious of its visual impact

Revealuxion, Kieun Kim

2015 Student work 'Revealuxion' by Kieun Kim combines brainwaves, LED lights, and finger movements into a wearable head garment that metamorphises, expands, rises according to the physical movement of the users hands and lights up according to their brainwaves in real-time. Where it is a deeply personal, introspective device, the physical performance latent within its form is a key example to me of the way physical computing can be expressed - it challenges my concieved notions of rigidity, cyber-aesthetic that i would previously conceived the medium as.

The Dots, Jack Lee

This 2023 work by Jack lee, 'The Dots' is a kinetic, arduino project that uses servo motors and sensors to change the transparency of polarised panels based on viewer proximity. Rather than an introspective work, this installation physically reflects the world around it, and changes according to it. This is a beautiful example of the possibility of physical computing and its relevance in reflecting the world around it.
